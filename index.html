Lee and Hunter's Wedding


Wedding Announcement
We are getting married Saturday, January 7th, 2023 at Harding's Restaurant in New York City.

The venue is in the Flatiron District of Manhattan, at 32 E 21st St, between 5th Avenue and Park Avenue.
The wedding will start at 6 PM. There will be a brief ceremony, then light snacks until 7:30, and then a meal.

Where should we stay?
We don't have a hotel with special rates for the wedding.
We recommend finding housing early, especially if you would like to stay in the Flatiron District itself.
One nearby hotel is SpringHill Suites Midtown Manhattan/Park Avenue.

You may find roomier lodging if you opt to stay in Queens or Brooklyn, which are a quick subway or taxi ride to the wedding.
If you are interested in splitting a hotel room with someone but don't know who to coordinate with, e-mail us.

How do we RSVP?
Once you know you are coming, please send us an e-mail with your name and the names of all your guests.
If you are not able to come, let us know by e-mailing the same address!

What should we wear?
Please dress formally.

Is there a registry?
It is already a generous gift to us for you to come to our "destination wedding," and we ask that you refrain from getting us wedding presents.

Should we take pictures?
We will have a photographer for the ceremony only. Afterwards, please take lots of pictures and share them with us!

Can I bring a date?
Probably! Let us know if this is your plan as there is a limit on the total number of people.
Update! On several occasions, our initial invitation only went to one member of a couple, due to a coding error.
We are sorry for this -- needless to say, you are both invited.

Can we bring our children?
Children ten and above are welcome. Let us know about children under ten -- we don't have childcare and they might not have the best time.

How will we entertain ourselves in New York in the winter?
In addition to museums and plays, there is the famous skating rink near Rockefeller Center.
If you have a "contactless" credit card or Apple Pay, you don't even need to buy a subway card to hop on the subway anymore.
